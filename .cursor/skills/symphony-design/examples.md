# Symphony Voice: Examples

Side-by-side comparisons from the brand voice guide, adapted for product UI copy.

## Marketing-style vs Symphony-style

| Not this | Use this instead |
|----------|------------------|
| Our world-class platform leverages AI-powered synergies to deliver transformative hiring insights. | The live TA report. Filter by hub, compare to 2025, export to PDF. |
| Empower your team with a holistic view of talent acquisition metrics. | See every hire this quarter. Scoped to your hubs. Updated live. |
| Our solution helps streamline HR reporting workflows across the enterprise. | We replaced the manual report with a live dashboard. 10 stakeholders, one source of truth. |

## UI microcopy

### Empty states

- Good: `No hires in Sarajevo for March 2026 yet.`
- Bad: `There are currently no records to display.`

### Error messages

- Good: `We could not reach the Sheet. Showing the last snapshot from 2 hours ago.`
- Bad: `An error has occurred. Please try again later.`

### Confirmation dialogs

- Good: `Remove enis.kudo@symphony.is from admins?` with buttons `Remove admin` and `Keep as admin`.
- Bad: `Are you sure you want to proceed?` with buttons `OK` and `Cancel`.

### Buttons

- Good: `Save changes`, `Refresh now`, `Add user`, `Export PDF`.
- Bad: `Submit`, `OK`, `Apply`, `Action`.

### Audit log entries

- Good: `aida.jugo@symphony.is changed the role of enis.kudo@symphony.is from viewer to admin at 14:02 UTC.`
- Bad: `Role was updated.`

## Voice self-check on example PRD sentences

> "Symphony's TA team tracks hiring performance against salary benchmarks."

- Specific? Yes, names Symphony, TA, and the topic.
- Leads with the point? Yes.
- Senior engineer proud? Yes.
- Who is doing the thing? TA team.
- Sounds like us? Yes.

> "Our innovative solution empowers stakeholders to gain holistic visibility into hiring performance."

- Specific? No. `Innovative`, `solution`, `empowers`, `holistic` are placeholders. Rewrite.
- Result: "We give each stakeholder a live view of hiring, scoped to their hubs."

## Colour usage in practice

### Right

- Primary purple `#6c69ff` on CTA buttons, active nav state, the KPI "Total hires" accent strip.
- Red `#fe7475` only on the "Below mid-point" KPI accent and error banners.
- Yellow `#ffbe3d` only on "At mid-point" and warning banners.
- Light grey `#f4f5fb` for page background and card surfaces.

### Wrong

- Primary purple as body text.
- Peach `#f9dfc4` for a button label.
- Navy `#222453` for the report table's text colour.
- Light blue `#91afea` as a hover state on a primary button.

## When in doubt

Run the five-question checklist from [SKILL.md](SKILL.md). If the sentence or the visual fails any of them, rewrite.
